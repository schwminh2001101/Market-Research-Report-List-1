<p><p>Fluorescence Activated Cell Sorting (FACS) is a specialized technique used in cell biology to isolate and analyze specific cells based on their fluorescence characteristics. By utilizing fluorescently labeled antibodies that bind to specific cellular markers, FACS enables the simultaneous analysis of multiple parameters of individual cells as they pass through a laser-based detection system. This allows researchers to obtain detailed insights into cell populations, driving advancements in various fields including immunology, cancer research, and stem cell studies.</p><p>The Fluorescence Activated Cell Sorting Market is poised for significant growth, projected to expand at a CAGR of 10.00% during the forecast period. Technological innovations, such as multiplexing capabilities and enhanced automation, are also driving market growth, making FACS an indispensable tool for researchers and clinicians alike.
